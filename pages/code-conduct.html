IAFEI Code of Professional Conduct

Ethical standards and professional principles guiding financial executives worldwide

Preamble

The International Association of Financial Executives Institutes (IAFEI) is committed to the highest standards of professional conduct and ethical behavior. This Code of Professional Conduct establishes the fundamental principles that guide our members in their professional responsibilities and interactions.

Professional Behavior

Members must comply with relevant laws and regulations and avoid actions that discredit the profession.

Professional Responsibilities

To the Public

  • Maintain transparency in financial reporting and communications
  • Uphold the highest standards of financial management and reporting
  • Protect the public trust placed in the financial management profession

To Employers and Organizations

  • Act with loyalty and in the best interests of the organization
  • Avoid conflicts of interest and disclose potential conflicts
  • Use organizational resources responsibly and efficiently

To the Profession

  • Contribute to the development and advancement of the profession
  • Support the establishment and maintenance of professional standards
  • Treat colleagues with respect and professionalism

Specific Guidelines

Area Requirement Examples
Financial Reporting Ensure accuracy, completeness, and compliance with applicable standards IFRS compliance, accurate disclosures, timely reporting
Risk Management Identify, assess, and manage risks appropriately Internal controls, risk assessment, mitigation strategies
Corporate Governance Support effective governance structures and processes Board reporting, audit committee support, compliance programs
Technology & Data Protect data integrity and ensure appropriate use of technology Data security, system controls, technology governance

Compliance and Enforcement

IAFEI member organizations are expected to adopt and enforce this Code of Professional Conduct within their respective jurisdictions and membership communities. This includes:

  • Providing education and training on ethical standards
  • Monitoring compliance with professional standards
  • Investigating allegations of professional misconduct
  • Imposing appropriate sanctions for violations
  • Supporting members in ethical decision-making
