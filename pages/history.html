History of IAFEI

Over 50 years of advancing financial excellence worldwide

Origins

In the 1950s, most of the world was engaged in rebuilding efforts following World War II. The localized financial practices of pre-war days were perceived as inadequate to meet the evolving responsibilities in the new global environment. During this decade, delegations from more than 20 national institutes visited the Financial Executives Institute (FEI) in the USA to learn about their practices in finance and professional organization.

As the 1960s began, professionals in many parts of the world became convinced of the potential benefits of interaction with their colleagues in the financial discipline. This awareness led to the founding of national institutes in many countries, which found it useful to counsel with the US FEI concerning appropriate objectives, standards, and organizational techniques.

Key Milestones

1961

Mexican Institute Formation

The Mexican Institute of Financial Executives (IMEF) came into being, marking one of the first international expansions of the financial executives movement.

1965

European Expansion

The Association Nationale des Conseillers et Controleurs de Gestion was organized in France, followed by similar movements in Italy and West Germany.

1969

IAFEI Foundation

The First International Congress of Financial Executives took place in Marbella, Spain. IAFEI was officially founded with a legal basis in Switzerland. First Chairman: Joe Biscop (Belgium), First President: Herb Knortz (USA).

1970

Brussels Congress

At the second World Congress in Brussels, 55 representatives of 15 institutes formalized the creation of IAFEI and set the tone for future international collaboration.

1984

IASC Invitation

IAFEI was first invited to become a full member of the International Accounting Standards Committee (IASC), marking its growing influence in global financial standards.

1991

Ethics Committee

The Ethics Committee presented the first official paper on Ethics during the XXII World Congress in Cancun, Mexico, establishing professional conduct standards.

1995

IASC Membership

IAFEI became a voting member of the International Accounting Standards Committee, providing substantial corporate input for the first time in international standard setting.

2003

Asian Expansion

Japan Association for Chief Financial Officers (JACFO) joined IAFEI, followed by China Association of Chief Financial Officers (CACFO) in 2004, marking significant expansion into Asia.

2019

Golden Anniversary

IAFEI celebrated its 50th anniversary, marking five decades of advancing financial excellence worldwide and establishing itself as the premier global association for financial executives.

2020

Virtual Innovation

Conducted the first virtual World Congress, adapting to global challenges while maintaining professional development and networking opportunities for members worldwide.

Modern Era

Global Reach

Today, IAFEI represents financial executives from over 20 countries, spanning multiple continents and diverse economic environments.

Digital Transformation

Leading initiatives in digital finance, AI implementation, and sustainable reporting practices to prepare members for the future of finance.

Professional Standards

Continuing to set and maintain high professional and ethical standards while adapting to emerging challenges in global finance.

"From humble beginnings in 1969 to a global network of financial professionals, IAFEI continues to shape the future of financial management worldwide."
